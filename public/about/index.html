More About Me...

I am a self taught Web Developer. My journey into web development and coding began due to a combination of things and circumstances. Initially I would pinpoint it mostly to curiosity. Triggered by spending time in California’s Silicon Valley where my partner lives. From being inspired after reading Sheryl Sandberg’s book ‘Lean In’. And having been a casual travel blogger, I had the desire to spruce up the design of my travel blog website with the goal to having my own domain for it and more control. Thus began my journey into teaching myself with online resources and courses how to be a programmer.

In truth this passion project is still laying in wait on the cutting room floor so to speak, although not actually discarded, just postponed for now, while I have been busy building up my skills and experience and turning this new found love of coding into a career!
I am so grateful I started down this road, the journey has been so rewarding and I love the challenges and thrill of successes it brings day in, day out. I am a ‘why’ person, I love to know how things work, and I think that is part of the sense of achievement and accomplishment I get as I learn new things, the curious girl inside of me thrives on it!

My background prior to becoming a web developer ranged from numerous hospitality gigs spanning from highschool through to my University days, where I studied a Bachelor of Arts in Film and Screen Production at Griffith University in Australia. I have always enjoyed practical and hands-on work, often with a creative and/or technical element. Some years were then spent with my own business doing bookkeeping and tax compliance work, and prior to my transition to developer, I spent 5 years as a Travel Consultant for Flight Centre Travel Group, making customers holiday dreams come true.

Travel is still very close to my heart, I have most definitely been bitten by the travel bug, to which I don’t seek a cure. I absolutely love the way travelling can change you, and open up your eyes to new things, new experiences, different cultures and an appreciation for the unknown. We don’t know what we don’t know, and until some new experience or journey takes us to that place. No experience is ever a waste, as long as we take something from it, this goes for the good and the bad. I apply this to my coding and learning journey all the time.

My other hobbies include cycling, tennis, reading, movies, writing and cooking yummy and healthy foods (yes I believe the combination is absolutely possible!). I am always trying to one-up myself in the kitchen, and improve on each dish I prepare and learn from it for the next time. I’m very big on home cooking when possible, and including nutrient dense ingredients in each meal for better health, focus (for coding of course!) and longevity.

One of my favourite things about my journey of learning to code, is that I can see that it will be a life-long journey, the learning of new things will never really stop. There is so much to learn, so many different ways to improve my skills and build upon my foundation and then branch off in new directions when it feels right. In an ever changing industry, I may never, ever feel I have finished learning, and that is just part of the appeal.
